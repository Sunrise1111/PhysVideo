We presents PhysVideo, a novel training-free method for synthesizing videos with physically plausible motion. Abstract

Most existing video diffusion models struggle to generate physically plausible object motion, despite achieving high visual fidelity. To address this, we introduce PhysVideo, a novel, plug-and-play method that is universally applicable to improve the physical plausibility of videos generated by text-to-video or image-to-video diffusion models without additional training. Our key insight is to integrate physics simulation into the denoising process of video diffusion models, enabling realistic dynamics. To bridge the video diffusion model and the physics simulator, we project the motion trajectory from the 3D simulation space to the 2D image space for motion control. Building on this, we propose to reposition the object within the noisy video frames during the denoising process, ensuring that the object motion is controlled by physics simulation without compromising the visual fidelity. Extensive experiments demonstrate the effectiveness of our method in terms of improved physical plausibility.
