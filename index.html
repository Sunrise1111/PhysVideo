Training-Free Simulator-Driven Motion Control for Physics-Grounded Video Generation

Abstract

Recent advances in video generation have achieved high visual fidelity but continue to suffer from physical hallucinations, where generated motions defy real-world dynamics. To address this, we present a training-free framework that effectively integrates physics simulators into the denoising process of video diffusion models for physics-grounded video generation. Specifically, we align video and simulation spaces by SfM reconstruction, enabling the physics simulator to produce 3D motion trajectories for the video to be synthesized. These simulated 3D motion trajectories are then projected onto the 2D image plane to modulate the video latents via a dynamic mask strategy, ensuring the synthesized motion follows the dynamics simulation. Extensive experiments demonstrate that our method significantly outperforms existing baselines in physical consistency while maintaining competitive visual fidelity.

Experimental Result

We compare our method with two categories of baselines: state-of-the-art foundation video generation models (CogVideoX-2b and Wan2.1-1.3b ) and specialized physics-grounded video generation methods: LVD, PhyT2V, PhysGen, and VLIPP.
